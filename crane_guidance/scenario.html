SummaryðŸ”—

This scenario examines how crane operators can lift and deliver the cargo around the construction site.

ModelsðŸ”—

crane_logðŸ”—

This log contains the observed crane states corresponding to the cranes.

cranesðŸ”—

This model lists all the cranes available in the system.

DefinitionsðŸ”—

pickup locationðŸ”—

This is an IfcZone where the delivery (from on-site_logistics) needs to be picked up.

attaching slingerðŸ”—

The actor (from actor_management) who will attach the delivery (from on-site_logistics) to the crane at the pickup location.

detaching slingerðŸ”—

The actor (from actor_management) who will take off the delivery (from on-site_logistics) from the crane at the delivery location (from on-site_logistics).

A detaching slinger can be identical with a attaching slinger.

crane deliveryðŸ”—

A crane delivery defines how the delivery (from on-site_logistics) should be transported within the construction site by a crane.

Each crane delivery is associated with:

  • a pickup location and the corresponding one or more attaching slingers,
  • the delivery location (from on-site_logistics) and the corresponding one or more detaching slingers, and
  • the crane,
  • the crane supervisor, and
  • the crane operator

crane operatorðŸ”—

The crane operator is an operator (from on-site_logistics) who controls the crane.

crane supervisorðŸ”—

Crane supervisor is an actor (from actor_management) who helps the crane operator by guiding her during the delivery (from on-site_logistics).

She has the final authority.

crane coordinatorðŸ”—

The crane coordinator is an actor (from actor_management) who supervises that the crane operators do not crash into each other.

craneðŸ”—

The crane is defined as IfcTransportElement and stored in cranes.

Its state is tracked as crane state and stored in crane_log.

crane stateðŸ”—

The crane state captures the state of the crane, including:

  • a timestamp of the observation,
  • position of the trolley, and
  • the height of the hook block (if available).

ScenarioðŸ”—

As-plannedðŸ”—

The planner role (from on-site_logistics) specifies the deliveries (from on-site_logistics) (with additional information defined) as crane deliveries.site

As-observedðŸ”—

The position of the crane's arm is continuously tracked as crane state in crane_log.machine

DivergenceðŸ”—

The device will guide the crane operator and the crane supervisor to the delivery location (from on-site_logistics).

Since the crane delivery is very dynamic, we explicitly do not provide a mechanism to signal the attaching slingers and detaching slingers when the crane delivery arrived, as these actors would have a visual contact anyhow.

SchedulingðŸ”—

For a given time range, the planner role (from on-site_logistics) should be able to inspect the plan of the crane deliveries as association between the cranes, crane operators, crane supervisors and tasks (from scheduling) (if available).

site

SafetyðŸ”—

The crane coordinator ensures visually that the crane operators do not crash.

In addition, the system should warn the crane coordinator if two crane arms are about to collide.

site

AnalyticsðŸ”—

As we track the crane movements in crane_log, the system should report the crane utilisation over time.

The details are to be discussed during the implementation (e.g., what time units and how we can measure it).
