The as-planned BIM model provided after the planning phase and updated throughout the construction.

DefinitionsðŸ”—

installerðŸ”—

The person who installed an element.

Uniquely identifiable by a contact (unstructured, can be e-mail, but also something completely arbitrarily such as a phone number or a company name).

In BIM:

IfcActor

alerteeðŸ”—

A person who needs to be alerted on his/her current risk level.

elementðŸ”—

Any relevant building element from the plan/main. It can be associated with observed/point_cloud and observed/pictures.

In BIM:

IfcProduct

Every element needs to have a unique identifier.

points in the vicinityðŸ”—

A subset of the points in the point cloud close to element.

images of the elementðŸ”—

A subset of the images that look at the element.

We can compute their subset based on their coordinates and angle.

preventive resourceðŸ”—

Preventive resource is person who is in charge of the safety inside.

The preventive resource is in charge of a set of task.

Depending on the budget, there will be preventive resources in charge of one or more zones or the whole sitesite.

health and safety managerðŸ”—

Health & safety manager is in charge of the issues on the site. The person is the boss of the health & safety of the site.site

risk levelðŸ”—

This is the risk level according to different standards. Refers to a concrete standard in standard/risk_level.

fall riskðŸ”—

A fall risk is an abstract entity.

It can be tied to a zone or to an element, but it doesn't need to.

The fall risk has a validity time span (which can also be open-ended).

The fall risk lives in plan/main.

ScenarioðŸ”—

As-plannedðŸ”—

We just use the federated model in plan/main.

The plan/main data would need to be accessible from the office.site office

As-observedðŸ”—

All the observed data from observed/point_cloud and observed/pictures lives in the same coordinate system as the federated model in plan/main. The mismatch errors between the coordinates are possible (see the acceptance criterion coordinate_error).

The observed data, observed/point_cloud and observed/pictures, would need to be accessible from the office.site office

DivergenceðŸ”—

Start simple. The preventive resource and health and safety manager manually inspect the elements and report absent/ill-placed to the system.

Next step: QR code. The QR code references the identifier of the element. The observations of the QR code are stored in observed/qr_codes.

Anybody authorized can add an observation of a QR code.

SafetyðŸ”—

Specification of the fall risks. The preventive resource links the element with the installer.

The health and safety manager inputs initially the fall risks manually.planning

The preventive resource makes notes regarding an unsafe element. Both the health and safety manager and preventive resource can insert new and change existing fall risks accordingly.

Alerts. The safety status risk level is continuously displayed to alertees in real-time based on the current fall risks and their location.

The location of the actor is not stored in the backend. The update logic is running on the alertee's device (i.e., a smart-watch) which sends repeated queries to the backend. The backend does not track the alertee.

Test CasesðŸ”—

safely test proximity to dangerðŸ”—

We mount an user device on a long stick and come closer and closer to the danger zone. We observe the distance to the danger zone and verify that the alarm is triggered on an appropriate proximity (judged by the health and safety manager).

The system states as well as the device data are recorded and re-used for repeated automatic testing.

blind test removed itemðŸ”—

Remove a safety item and inform the workers. Check that the responsible person could find out that it is missing using the system.

analytics correct on an exampleðŸ”—

Record 3 complete states of the whole system and freeze them. Manually compute the expected analytics. Check that the output of the system coincides with the manually computed analytics.

Acceptance CriteriaðŸ”—

coordinate errorðŸ”—

Over repeated runs (e.g., over the days), there will be randomized errors between the coordinates in plan/main and observed/point_cloud and observed/pictures, including the drift and systematic errors.

We have to see after the experiments whether these errors are tolerable for this scenario.

Our expectation is that the error is below 1 meter, rarely 2 meters. We do not have precise statistics at the moment (2021-01-09).
